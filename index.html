Brief Biography

Raila Amolo Odinga was a titan of African politics whose life journey mirrored Kenya's struggle for independence and democratic maturity. A trained mechanical engineer, he transitioned from the lecture halls of the University of Nairobi to become the most influential opposition figure and later the Prime Minister of the Republic of Kenya.

Life Timeline

1945

Birth in Maseno

Born January 7, 1945, at CMS Hospital, Maseno. Son of Jaramogi Oginga Odinga, Kenya's first Vice President.

1950s - 1960s

Academic Foundation

Attended Kisumu Union Primary and Maranda High School. Later moved to East Germany for higher studies.

1970

University & Engineering

Graduated from the Technical University of Magdeburg with an MSc in Mechanical Engineering.

1973

Marriage to Ida

Married Ida Anyango Oyoo, who would become his pillar throughout his political struggles.

1974

Working at KEBS

Helped establish the Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS) and served as its Deputy Director.

1982 - 1991

Coup Attempt & Detention

Accused of involvement in the 1982 coup attempt. Spent nearly a decade in and out of detention without trial, becoming a symbol of resistance.

1991 - 1992

The Second Liberation

Joined FORD to successfully fight for the repeal of Section 2A, ushering in multi-party democracy in Kenya.

1997

First Presidential Bid

Ran for president for the first time under the National Development Party (NDP), finishing third.

2002

"Kibaki Tosha"

Endorsed Mwai Kibaki at Uhuru Park, a heroic move that united the opposition and ended KANU's 40-year rule.

2005

The Orange Revolution

Led the 'No' (Orange) camp to victory in the Constitutional Referendum, leading to the birth of the ODM party.

2007 - 2008

Election Conflict & GNU

Disputed presidential results led to a crisis. Following the National Accord, he became the Prime Minister in a Grand Coalition Government.

2013

CORD Coalition Bid

Ran for the presidency under the CORD coalition. The Supreme Court upheld Uhuru Kenyatta's victory.

2017

Nullified Results & Resistance

Won a historic Supreme Court petition nullifying the election. He later boycotted the repeat poll.

2018

The Handshake

Stunned the nation by reconciling with President Uhuru Kenyatta for the sake of national unity, leading to the BBI process.

2018 - 2023

AU High Representative

Served as the African Union High Representative for Infrastructure Development in Africa.

2022

Azimio la Umoja Bid

His fifth presidential bid under the Azimio coalition. Narrowly lost to William Ruto according to IEBC results.

2024 - 2025

AUC Chair Campaign

Launched a continental campaign for the Chairperson of the African Union Commission, garnering support across Africa.

2025

Final Transition

Passed away in October 2025, leaving a legacy as the primary architect of Kenya's democratic space.
