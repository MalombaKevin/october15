Brief Biography

Raila Amolo Odinga was a titan of African politics whose life journey mirrored Kenya's struggle for independence and democratic maturity. A trained mechanical engineer, he transitioned from the lecture halls of the University of Nairobi to become the most influential opposition figure and later the Prime Minister of the Republic of Kenya.

JOURNEY OF A STATESMAN

1945

Birth in Maseno

Born January 7, 1945, at CMS Hospital, Maseno. Son of Mary Juma and Jaramogi Oginga Odinga.

1950s - 1970

Academic Foundations

Attended Kisumu Union Primary, Maranda Primary, and Maranda High School. Later studied in East Germany at the Herder Institute and Magdeburg Technical University (MSc Mechanical Engineering, 1970).

1970

University Lecturing

Returned to Kenya and became a Lecturer at the University of Nairobi (Mechanical Engineering) at age 25.

1973

Marriage to Ida

Married Ida Oyoo, marking the beginning of a life-long partnership in family and activism.

1974

Appointment at KEBS

Appointed Deputy Director of the Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS), contributing to national industrial standards.

1982

The 1982 Coup & Detention

Accused of involvement in the August 1 attempted coup. He was detained without trial for 6 years, becoming a symbol of resistance.

1990 - 1992

Multi-Party Democracy

Detained for a third time in 1990. Released in 1991, fled to Norway, then returned to join FORD in the 1992 struggle for multi-partyism.

2002

Kibaki Tosha!

Joined NARC and endorsed Mwai Kibaki with the famous "Kibaki Tosha" declaration, ending KANU's 24-year rule.

2005

The Orange Movement

Led the 'NO' campaign (Orange) against the proposed constitution, defeating the government and birthing the ODM party.

2007 - 2013

Government of National Unity

Following the disputed 2007 polls, became the 2nd Prime Minister of Kenya in a power-sharing Grand Coalition Government.

2017

The Disputed Election

Contested the presidency under the NASA coalition. The Supreme Court nullified the first poll; he boycotted the repeat, later being sworn in as "The People's President."

2018

The Handshake

In a historic move on March 9, 2018, he entered a peace pact (The Handshake) with President Uhuru Kenyatta to unite the country.

2018 - 2023

AU High Representative

Appointed as the African Union High Representative for Infrastructure Development in Africa, championing continental connectivity.

2022

Azimio la Umoja

Contested his fifth presidential election under the Azimio coalition, losing narrowly to William Ruto.

2025

Passing of a Giant

Passed away on October 15, 2025, in Kochi, India, after a long life of service. Interred in his ancestral home in Bondo.
